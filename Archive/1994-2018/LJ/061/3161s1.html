Acknowledgments

Here is a rogues' gallery of the people I know who share the blame for this project and are part of the Creat team whether they like it or not. They have certainly made this a truly international project. My thanks to:

Thomas Nau from Ulm University for dreaming up the circuit design and writing the PCB program and for the left over PCB that was our first pre-prototype.

Harry Eaton from John Hopkins University for its continued maintenance.

Jeff Dionne from Ryerson Polytechnic University in Canada for the gcc6811 package.

Jerome Debard from Toulouse for the work toward the first prototype and the C-runtime.

Daniel Roques Escolar and Alberto Ramos Fernandez from UPV Valencia for additional hardware refinements, the make system, LCD and keyboard modules and the huge manual (especially Danny, who came back to England at his own expense to finish the documentation).
